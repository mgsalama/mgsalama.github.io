About Me

Before coming to University of California, Berkeley, I knew that I wanted to find a major and career path in which the work I did could benefit people or the environment. I really enjoyed statistics but had never coded before, yet I was really drawn to computer science. After taking my first data science courses at Cal, I was interested in the ability to combine working with data as well as writing code, and I was especially impressed when I saw just how useful data science could be in many different industries. I had found the major for me!

Although my specialization for the data science major is in economics, I have been taking classes and researching on my own about the different fields that data science could be used in order to progress towards solutions for major medical or environmental issues that affect our world and its population greatly today. Because of this, I hope to eventually work in an industry or for an organization where I can help work towards these solutions that can benefit our planet and its people.

Aside from my interests in data science and using data science to help leave the world in a better state than I found it, I am also studying and minoring in Chinese at Cal. I also want to start self-learning more computer science and software engineering fundamentals after graduation in order to expand my skill set. In my free time, I enjoy reading, creating video content for fun, and spending time with my family, friends, and dogs.
